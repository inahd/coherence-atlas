# Vision — Coherence Atlas

Coherence Atlas is an open cultural infrastructure: a **local-first research backend** that can grow into a **living cultural intelligence platform**. Its purpose is to map relationships across traditional knowledge systems and the human practices they inform—without collapsing diversity into a single muddy ontology.

## North Star
A shared ecosystem where:
- scholars visualize relationships across texts, time cycles, places, rituals, plants, and arts
- musicians explore time-based ragas and rhythmic geometry
- practitioners generate practice sequences (yoga, breathwork, seasonal routines)
- designers generate yantras/mandalas and translate them into crafts, architecture, and landscapes
- communities collaborate on evidence, overlays (tradition variants), and canon maturation

## Core principle
**Preserve and map traditional knowledge first. Applications come later.**  
Atlas prioritizes provenance, coherence, and the ability to evolve toward stability.

---

## The Atlas as layered infrastructure
Atlas is intentionally modular: each layer can be built independently, but all layers share the same relational substrate.

### Layer 1 — Knowledge Graph Engine (archive layer)
A graph linking:
- texts and passages (with locators)
- concepts and entities (deities, plants, places, rituals, festivals, music, architecture)
- celestial/time systems (panchanga dimensions)

This is the kernel: if this is strong, everything else becomes possible.

### Layer 2 — Exploration Interface (interactive relationships)
Interactive navigation and search:
- cluster exploration (“show the Rohini constellation of connected domains”)
- timeline and calendar views (seasonal/ritual cycles)
- filters by truth layer (canon vs inference vs overlays)

### Layer 3 — Practice + Creative layers (generators)
Once the graph exists, generators become natural:
- worksheets, curriculum, study paths
- rituals/calendars/plans
- art exports (yantras/mandalas, pattern sets)
- simulations/games as accessible front ends

## Primary index system (the coherence backbone)
Atlas uses **time cycles as coordinates** (so domains can align without forcing a single taxonomy):

**Panchanga / Jyotish dimensions**
- Nakshatra
- Tithi
- Graha
- Rashi
- Vara  
(+ optional: Yoga, Karana, Muhurta, etc.)

These provide the “clock + coordinate system” that many Vedic sciences already share.

Example (prototype story):
> Moon → Rohini → deity → plants → rituals → sacred sites → music/time-of-day → practice/craft outputs

## Domain modules (the integrated sophistication)
These are the major “systems” your vision calls for (each can be built as a pack that writes/reads the same graph):

1. **Devotional Media Commons**  
   Copyleft repository for recordings, manuscripts, lectures, art, oral histories.

2. **Sacred Geometry Engine**  
   Generative yantras/mandalas/lotus/star-polygon systems; recursive pattern grammars.

3. **Craft Pattern Generators**  
   Geometry → weaving drafts / embroidery charts / crochet patterns / mosaics / carving templates.

4. **Music & Sound Systems (Gandharvaveda-inspired)**  
   Raga explorer; rhythm geometry mapping; mantra resonance analysis; time-based composition.

5. **Yoga & Body Practice Systems**  
   Asana library linked to chakras/therapeutics/breathwork; time-of-day/seasonal routines.

6. **Time Engine (Jyotish layer)**  
   Lunar cycles, transits, festival calendars, auspicious timing; temporal context for all domains.

7. **Permaculture GIS Layer**  
   Watershed/swale/orchard design; regenerative planning; mandala gardens tied to ecology and geometry.

8. **Vastu Architecture Generator**  
   Homes/temples/villages oriented by direction, solar path, terrain; symbolic geometry integration.

9. **Cultural Simulation Environment**  
   Village ecology, agriculture cycles, ritual economies, pilgrimage flows—sandbox experimentation.

10. **Interactive Game Layer**  
    Data-driven cultural sims (village management, sacred geometry puzzles, ecology balancing).

11. **Spiritual Economy Layer**  
    Funding primitives for translations, rituals, artists, preservation—supporting cultural labor.

12. **Creative Production Layer**  
    Art/music/fiction/educational materials generated from the atlas and linked back as artifacts.

## What “success” looks like
### Early proof (prototype)
A small seed atlas (~100 nodes) already demonstrates:
- the panchanga backbone
- one or two rich clusters (e.g., Rohini, Bharani, or a tithi-nitya cluster)
- interactive exploration + basic generators (wiki pages, simple worksheets)

### Mature form
A coherent cultural operating system where coherence *emerges*:
- communities add evidence and overlays
- the system proposes coherent growth and highlights conflicts
- canon becomes a stable, minimal “common core” while overlays preserve diversity

---

## Design philosophy
This project assumes cultural systems once contained internal coherence linking:
- land, craft, architecture, music, ritual, seasonal cycles, community organization

Atlas does not enforce perfect coherence. It builds tools so coherence can **emerge**, stabilize, and remain legible even when variants exist.

---

## How to help (developer-facing)
If you want to contribute, the biggest leverage points are:
- ingestion quality (text-first, OCR where needed, dedupe + locators)
- evidence matching and canonical promotion gates
- resolver improvements (type constraints, conflict resolution, cluster settling)
- better exploration UX (filters, collapsible clusters, entity-centric pages)
- domain packs that add structured datasets without muddying truth layers
